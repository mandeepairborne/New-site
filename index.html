Medium modification of Pentaquark Θ+

NITJ

research

The exotic pentaquark baryon Θ+ with quark content uudds ̄ is studied theoreically using a chiral SU(3) quark mean field model to investigate medium modifi- cations in dense nuclear and strange matter. The in-medium mass of Θ+ exhibits a substantial drop with increasing baryon density. The systematically lower values in strange matter suggest greater stability for pentaquarks in hyperonic cores of neutron stars. The pronounced medium modifications provide insights into altered hadron structure and partial chiral restoration in dense astrophysical environments, with implications for possible Θ+ phases inside neutron stars.

What is playing by ear, for mathematics?

Apr 2, 2023

In the context of music, "playing by ear" describes a performer's ability to improvise melodies and harmonies without relying on formal notation. I'm curious about whether such an intuitive sense of mathematical structure exists, and how it might manifest.

Code review should use a directed graph, not a linked list

Aug 2, 2022

Code review tools like GitHub, GitLab, and Bitbucket are fundamentally limited by their reliance on the linked list model of threaded discussion. I propose that a directed graph would be a much better abstraction for capturing the dynamic structure of code reviews, and would allow for much richer and more flexible discourse.
